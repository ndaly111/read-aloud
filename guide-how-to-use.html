How to Use Read‑Aloud (Step‑by‑Step)

Last updated: December 2025

Quick start (30 seconds)

  1. Go to the Read‑Aloud tool.
  2. Paste your text into the main text box.
  3. Select a Language and Voice (or keep the defaults).
  4. Adjust Speed if you want (0.9× is a good starting point for studying).
  5. Press Start.

What each control does

  • Language / Voice: choose the voice your browser/device provides for that language.
  • Speed: slower helps catch details; faster helps skim for gist.
  • Start: begins reading from the top (or from your current position, depending on your browser behavior).
  • Pause / Resume: stop and continue without losing your place.
  • Stop: ends playback.

Pick a voice that matches your task

  • Studying: a clear, neutral voice + slightly slower speed (0.85–0.95×).
  • Proofreading: neutral voice + 0.9× to catch missing words and repeated phrases.
  • Language learning: target-language voice + slower speed at first; increase over time.

Tip: preview options on the Voice Gallery and pick one that feels “invisible” (not distracting).

Best practices for longer text

  • Chunk it: break huge documents into sections (headings/paragraph blocks).
  • Use two passes: pass 1 for understanding, pass 2 for details.
  • Keep your “source” document open: if you’re editing while listening, fix the original and replay that section.

If you’re on iPhone/iPad

Mobile browsers can be stricter about audio playback. Make sure you tap Start (a user action) to begin audio, and check your device volume. If you still get “no sound” or “no voices,” use the Help page troubleshooting section.

Privacy note (simple rule)

If the text is sensitive, treat it like you would any clipboard content: don’t paste private text on shared devices, and avoid pasting while screen-sharing. For details, see Privacy‑First Text‑to‑Speech.
