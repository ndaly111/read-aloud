Proofread Your Writing by Ear

When you read a draft silently, your brain auto-corrects missing words and glosses over clumsy phrasing. Listening to your text forces every syllable through a fresh channel—ideal for spotting hidden errors.

Quick setup: Paste your draft into the home page, choose a neutral voice, set the speed to 0.95×, and press Start. Keep your text on screen so you can pause and edit in real time.

Five errors you’ll catch by listening

  1. Double words (e.g., “the the”)—sounds like a stutter.
  2. Missing articles (“go to store”)—voice highlights the gap.
  3. Run-on sentences—you’ll hear yourself run out of breath.
  4. Tongue-twisters / awkward rhythm—rewrite for flow.
  5. Inconsistent tense or POV—shifts jump out aloud.

Workflow used by professional editors

  • Pass 1: Listen to full piece, jot quick notes only.
  • Pass 2: Fix notes, then listen again at 1.1× to check pacing.
  • Final pass: Export an MP3 and proof it on your phone during a walk.

Pro tips

  • Switch to a different accent for final proof—it forces fresh attention.
  • Record yourself reading and compare. Robot catch ⇾ human nuance.
  • Add a one-second pause after every “. to slow things just enough.
