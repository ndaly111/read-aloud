Proofread by Ear

Listening reveals issues that silent reading misses. Use these steps to turn Read‑Aloud into a reliable editing companion.

Setup

  • Open the tool and paste 400–800 words from your draft.
  • Pick a voice that differs from your own so mistakes stand out.
  • Set speed to 0.95–1.05× depending on density.
  • Bookmark the page so you can run a quick listen after every writing session.

While listening

Keep a notepad for quick tags such as [awk] for awkward sentences or [ex] where you owe an example. Pause with Ctrl+Shift+P when you hear something odd, jot the tag, then resume.

If you are editing dialogue, note the character name beside each tag so you can review voice consistency later.

After the pass

Return to your draft and resolve tags in order: cut filler words, swap verbs, and add missing examples. Run a final listen at a slightly faster speed to catch lingering issues.

For long projects, keep a running list of phrases you overuse. The next time you listen, keep that list nearby and pause whenever you hear a repeat. Over time you will build your own personal style guide that shortens future editing sessions.

Before publishing, listen to your introduction one more time on mobile speakers to ensure the hook sounds clear.
