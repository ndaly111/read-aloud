Listen to Your Study Materials Anywhere

Reading dense textbooks for hours can fry your attention span. With Read-Aloud you can flip the workflow: press play, close your eyes, and let the information sink in while you commute, cook, or rest your eyes. Here’s the two-minute method used by medical, engineering and language-arts students.

1Collect your sources

  • Copy lecture notes straight from OneNote, Google Docs or Notion.
  • Use Print → Save as PDF to export slides or articles.
  • For a long PDF, extract one chapter at a time with Preview › New from Clipboard (macOS) or PDF24 Tools online.

2Paste & select a voice

Return to the home page, paste your chunk into the box, then:

  1. Pick a language & accent that feels natural (e.g. US-English Female).
  2. Set speed around 0.9× for heavy conceptual material; up to 1.3× for revision quick-runs.

3Use spaced-repetition playback

Research shows that alternating active recall with passive listening improves long-term retention.1

  • After each major heading, hit Pause and recite what you just heard.
  • Replay trickier segments at 0.8×.
  • Finish with a 1.5× speed skim of the whole chapter the night before your test.

FAQ for students

Will this cost me anything?
No. The tool is free; lightweight ads cover hosting.
Can I download the audio?
Yes—click the Save MP3 button under the player to keep a copy for offline listening.
Is my text stored on a server?
No. All speech is generated locally in your browser; nothing leaves your device.
