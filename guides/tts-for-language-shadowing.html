Shadowing Practice with TTS: Scripts, Routines, and How to Track Progress

For language learners who want consistent pronunciation practice without extra apps. Works with any system voice installed on your device.

Pick scripts that actually teach you

Choose 150–250 word scripts that mirror real conversations: news blurbs, short dialogues, or paragraphs from graded readers. Avoid tongue twisters at first; clarity matters more than speed. Paste one script at a time into the tool and label your notebook with the script title and date.

Shadowing routine (15 minutes)

  1. Listen cold: Play the script once at normal speed with no speaking. Circle words you do not recognize.
  2. Slow mimic: Lower speed to 0.85–0.9×. Speak quietly with the voice, matching rhythm and intonation.
  3. Normal pace: Return to 1.0× and shadow again. Focus on linking words and matching pauses.
  4. Record yourself: Use your phone or laptop recorder for a 20–30 second snippet. Compare to the system voice and note differences.
  5. Finish fast: If you feel comfortable, try 1.1× to test your breath control. If it feels sloppy, drop back to 1.0× and repeat.

Keep each routine short so you stop before fatigue sets in. Two routines per day beat one long session you abandon mid-week.

Choosing the right voice

Pick a voice that matches your target accent. Many system voices include region labels (US, UK, AU, ES-MX, FR-CA). If you are practicing tonal languages, choose a clear, steady voice and slow to 0.9× to hear tone contours. The Voice Gallery lists how to install additional voices on each platform.

If your device only offers one voice, alternate between browsers. Chrome and Edge sometimes expose different voices even on the same system.

Track progress without spreadsheets

  • Create a simple log in your notes app: date, script title, speed used, and one thing to improve.
  • Record the same script once a week. Listen to week-old recordings to hear changes in pacing and pronunciation.
  • Set a monthly goal such as “shadow 20 scripts” or “hold 1.0× speed without stumbling.”
  • Use the progress meter in Read‑Aloud as a built-in timer—finish the bar, then rest.

Common issues and fixes

  • Voice too fast even at 1.0×: Switch to a different voice or browser; some voices are recorded faster.
  • Background noise in recordings: Use wired headphones with a built-in mic or move closer to the device.
  • Words run together: Insert blank lines between sentences in the textarea so pauses are clearer.
  • Stamina drops mid-script: Break the script into two halves and shadow separately.
