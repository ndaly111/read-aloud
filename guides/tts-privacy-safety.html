Privacy & Safety: What Not to Paste, How Local Voices Work, and Safer Habits

Read‑Aloud runs entirely in your browser, but privacy still depends on smart habits. Use this guide to decide what is safe to paste and how to reduce risk.

Local voices, not cloud streaming

Read‑Aloud asks your browser for system voices and queues text through the Speech Synthesis API. No text is uploaded to our servers or to cloud TTS providers. Analytics track page visits only. This design keeps your documents local and works even when offline once the page is loaded.

What not to paste

  • Unredacted medical notes or protected health information.
  • Student records with names, IDs, or grades.
  • Contracts containing confidential client terms.
  • Passwords, API keys, or private links.

If you must listen to sensitive material, replace names with placeholders before pasting and delete the text after listening. Avoid using shared or public computers for confidential work.

Safer habits while listening

  • Use a private window if you share a device. Close the tab when finished.
  • Keep the tool in the foreground to prevent screenshots from other apps.
  • Use wired headphones in public spaces so audio is not overheard.
  • When copying from a website, strip tracking parameters from URLs before saving notes.

Backing up without exporting audio

Because the Speech Synthesis API does not provide MP3s, you should store your notes, not the audio itself. Save the cleaned text and your listening notes in a secure document. If you need an audio record, use your operating system’s screen recorder with system audio while ensuring you have rights to the material.

Sharing devices and classrooms

In classrooms or libraries, log out of personal accounts and avoid pasting private text. If Read‑Aloud is part of an accommodation, coordinate with your instructor so they know audio is for accessibility, not distraction. Use the Help Center if voices are locked down on managed devices.

Data retention

Read‑Aloud stores no pasted text. Your browser may keep the page in memory until you close it. For extra assurance, refresh the tab or close the browser after a session. Ads run only on content pages like this guide; the tool intentionally avoids loading ad scripts next to user text.
